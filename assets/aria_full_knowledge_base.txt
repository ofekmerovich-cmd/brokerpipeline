===============================================================================
QUALIFICATION QUESTIONS (ask in order)
===============================================================================

  Ask these naturally in conversation. Don't rapid-fire them.
  Listen to answers — they fuel objection handling and closing.

  1. "What types of insurance do you primarily sell?"
     → Helps tailor the pitch. P&C brokers care about volume.
       Life brokers care about quality. Commercial brokers care about deal size.

  2. "How are you currently getting new clients?"
     → Identifies pain points. Most say referrals + cold calling.
       If they mention buying leads, ask about quality/exclusivity.

  3. "How many new clients do you typically bring on per month?"
     → Establishes baseline. Most independent brokers say 3-8.
       This number becomes the "before" in the ROI pitch.

  4. "What's your biggest challenge right now when it comes to growing
     your book?"
     → Open-ended. Let them talk. Their answer IS your selling point.
       Common: "not enough time," "leads are garbage," "too much competition."

  5. "If you could add 15 qualified appointments to your calendar every
     month, how would that impact your business?"
     → Future-pacing. Gets them to visualize success with BrokerPipeline.
       Whatever they say, agree and amplify: "Exactly. That's what we deliver."

BROKER ECONOMICS:
───────────────────────────────────────────

  Average independent broker income:     $80,000 - $200,000/year
  Average book of business:              200 - 500 clients
  Average client retention rate:         85% - 90%
  Average policy annual premium:         $1,500 - $3,000
  Commission rates (new business):       10% - 15%
  Commission rates (renewals):           5% - 10%
  Average client lifetime value:         $8,000 - $15,000 (5-7 year retention)
  Cost to acquire a client (industry):   $300 - $800
  BrokerPipeline cost per appointment:   ~$60 - $100 (at Starter plan)


COMMON PAIN POINTS:
───────────────────────────────────────────

  - Spending 3+ hours/day cold calling with <5% connection rate
  - Buying shared leads that go to 5-10 other brokers simultaneously
  - Inconsistent lead flow — feast or famine month to month
  - Competition from online direct insurers (Geico, Progressive, Lemonade)
  - Difficulty standing out in a crowded local market
  - Too much time on admin/paperwork instead of selling
  - Aging book of business with declining retention
  - Difficulty recruiting and training new producers
  - Rising customer acquisition costs
  - Pressure from carriers to hit production minimums

REGULATORY KNOWLEDGE:
───────────────────────────────────────────

  - Each state requires a separate insurance license
  - Continuing education (CE) required annually (varies by state, 20-40 hours)
  - E&O (Errors & Omissions) insurance required or strongly recommended
  - Independent brokers can represent multiple carriers (our target)
  - Captive agents work for one carrier only (NOT our target market)
  - Surplus lines require separate license in most states
  - NAIC (National Association of Insurance Commissioners) sets guidelines
  - State DOI (Department of Insurance) regulates at state level
